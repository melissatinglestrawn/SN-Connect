Zweig Group · Strategic Partnership
SN + Zweig Group: Stronger Together for AEC

I grew up on a quintessential 120-acre corn farm in Iowa. My dad (whom I probably admired more than anyone on the planet) used to say at every harvest, without fail: “You can’t fake a good crop.”

What he meant by that was simple: real results show. You don’t get them from talk, optics, or shortcuts. You get them from hard, consistent work—cultivation, care, and time. If all that’s working, people can see it. If it’s not, they can see that too.

That’s the analogy I keep coming back to as I look at the SN + ZG partnership one year in: a good crop, built on real effort, real care, and real collaboration.

Over the past 12 months, our two firms have done more than just “partner.” We’ve worked side by side across marketing, advisory, events, data, and the behind-the-scenes operational lift that makes it all actually work—and gel. And it’s showing up in results the industry can’t help but notice.

What we’re building together is something different: a new standard for what clients should expect—and a combination AEC hasn’t seen before. The buzz isn’t subtle anymore. It’s building. It’s real. And it’s exciting.

A shared vision to elevate the industry.

So… yeah… like Dad said—you can’t fake this.

On behalf of all my colleagues at Zweig Group, a sincere thank you to the SN team for the way you’ve embraced us. You didn’t just make space—you made us feel genuinely welcomed. That’s a big reason this has worked as well as it has.

Thanks to everyone—ZG-ers and SN-ers alike—for the work, the attitude, and the shared commitment to doing this the right way. It shows.

Here’s to a very bright future.
We’re not just claiming success—we’re demonstrating it.

#BetterTogether

Jeremy

Running on Deadlines, Determination, and Questionable Amounts of Caffeine
April doesn't arrive — it hits. And for our Tax team, busy season is less a time of year and more a full-body experience. Since December, they've been operating at a pace that suggests sleep is optional and coffee is a core system dependency.
  • →Reassigned and stabilized a large client portfolio
  • →Onboarded new clients and three new team members right before the surge
  • →Launched Resource Planner and rolled out new processes and tools — mid-flight
  • →Built a centralized hub for book and tax depreciation
  • →Continued pushing automation and standardization — because doing things the easy way is apparently the goal… eventually
The volume is real. But what's more impressive is how it's getting done: quick pivots, constant collaboration, and a shared understanding that "I've got it" might actually mean "we've got it."
They're not at the finish line yet — but momentum is strong, spirits are (mostly) intact, and the work keeps moving. For now, it's one deadline at a time. But somewhere out there: vacations, gardens, boats, pools… and a vague memory of what weekends used to feel like.
